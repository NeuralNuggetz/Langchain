Cricket is a bat-and-ball sport played between two teams of eleven players.  The game centers on a 22-yard pitch with a wicket (three stumps topped by two bails) at each end. One team bats, aiming to score runs, while the other bowls and fields to dismiss the batters. Runs are scored primarily by the two batters on the field swapping ends after the ball is hit, with each successful exchange counting as one run. Hitting the ball to the boundary scores four runs if it bounces first, or six runs if it clears the boundary on the full. The fielding team gets batters out in several ways, including bowled (hitting the stumps), caught (catching the ball before it bounces), run out (breaking the wicket while the batter is running), and LBW (Leg Before Wicket).  An innings ends when ten batters are out or a set number of overs (six-ball sequences) are completed. The team with the most runs at the end of both innings wins.
